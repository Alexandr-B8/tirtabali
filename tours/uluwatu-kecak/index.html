Uluwatu & Kecak

An afternoon on the dramatic Bukit cliffs — a hidden white-sand beach, the clifftop Uluwatu temple at golden hour, the hypnotic Kecak fire dance over the sea, and a toes-in-the-sand seafood dinner at Jimbaran.

Overview

Bali's most spectacular sunset, with the fire dance to match.

The southern Bukit peninsula is where Bali turns vertical — limestone cliffs dropping to turquoise water. We start with a relaxed afternoon at one of the hidden beaches, then head up to the clifftop Uluwatu temple as the light turns gold.

As the sun drops, the Kecak fire dance unfolds in the temple's open-air amphitheatre — a hundred chanting voices and a fire-lit retelling of the Ramayana, with the sea ablaze behind. Afterwards, dinner is fresh seafood on the sand at Jimbaran. Pure magic.

Good to know

Activity level

Easy — gentle walking, some steps at the beach and temple.

What to bring

A swimsuit for the beach, light cover for the temple (sarongs provided) and a camera.

Best time

Dry season for the clearest sunsets; we watch the forecast either way.

Good to know

Hold onto sunglasses and hats at the temple — the monkeys are notorious.

Good to ask

Uluwatu & Kecak — your questions

Do we need to book the Kecak dance ahead?
Yes, it sells out — we reserve good seats for you as part of the tour.
Is the temple visit suitable for everyone?
Yes, it's gentle walking on cliff paths. Just mind the monkeys and keep belongings zipped away.
Can dinner suit dietary needs?
Of course — tell us about any allergies or preferences and we'll arrange it at Jimbaran.
What should we wear at the temple?
Shoulders and knees covered; sarongs are provided and included.
